# LinkedIn Post Version

We would rather you pick the right tool than pick ours for the wrong reasons.

There are three AI agent platforms getting attention right now: OpenClaw, NVIDIA NemoClaw, and our own Bonobots. We wrote an honest comparison because we think the industry needs fewer vendor hit pieces and more real talk.

Here is the short version:

OpenClaw is a genuinely great personal AI assistant. It connects to WhatsApp, runs on your machine, and does real work. If you want a personal AI sidekick, it is hard to beat. We actually use it ourselves.

NVIDIA NemoClaw takes OpenClaw and adds privacy guardrails and local model execution via Nemotron. If data sovereignty is your top priority and you have NVIDIA hardware, it solves a real problem.

But neither was built for enterprise teams deploying AI agents at scale.

That is what Bonobots does.

Multi-provider routing across AWS Bedrock, Azure OpenAI, GCP Vertex AI, OpenAI, Anthropic, and Groq. If one provider goes down at 2 AM, your agents keep running. Cross-region inference profiles that no one else offers. Cost optimization that routes cheap tasks to cheap models and hard tasks to frontier models. Compliance tooling mapped to SOC2, HIPAA, GDPR, and ISO27001. Agent orchestration where specialists delegate to each other using different models.

We are honest about our gaps too. No local model execution yet. Younger product. Smaller team than NVIDIA. We wrote all of this in the full comparison.

The question is not which platform is "best." The question is what you are building. If the answer is enterprise AI agents, we think Bonobots is the right choice.
