Reference Architecture

Creative AI Pipeline

A framework for structuring multi-model AI workflows in creative production — from brief intake to published asset.

The 6-Stage Pipeline

Every creative AI workflow follows the same general arc. The models, tools, and review gates change by use case — the shape stays consistent.

1

Brief & Intake

Structured input: brand, audience, tone, format, constraints. This becomes the "contract" every downstream agent works from.

2

Research & Context

RAG retrieval of brand guidelines, past campaign performance, competitor creative, audience data. Grounds generation in reality.

3

Ideation

LLM generates concepts, headlines, angles, storyboards. Multiple variants produced in parallel for selection.

4

Production

Specialised models produce the actual assets — images, video, voiceover, copy. Each model type handles its domain.

5

Review & QA

Automated checks (brand compliance, tone, legal) plus human approval gates. The critical trust layer before anything goes live.

6

Publish & Learn

Distribute to channels, track performance, feed results back into the pipeline for the next iteration.

Orchestration Patterns

How you wire agents together matters as much as which models you pick. Four patterns cover most creative workflows.

Sequential

Chain / Waterfall

Each agent's output feeds the next. Simple, predictable. Good for linear workflows like brief → copy → design → review.

Parallel + Merge

Fan-out / Fan-in

One agent fans work to many specialists (copy, image, video), then a merge agent assembles the final package. Fast for multi-asset campaigns.

Supervisor

Creative Director

An orchestrator agent delegates, reviews, and re-routes. Can send work back for revision. Most flexible, highest token cost.

Critic Loop

Generator + Evaluator

One agent creates, another scores against criteria (brand voice, compliance). Loops until quality threshold is met. Self-improving output.

Model Categories

A creative pipeline typically spans 4–5 model types. No single provider covers everything — multi-provider routing is essential.

  • Language models (LLMs) — Copy, scripts, strategy, ideation, review. GPT-4o, Claude, Llama, Gemini. The backbone of every pipeline stage.
  • Image generation — DALL-E 3, Stable Diffusion (via Bedrock), Imagen (Vertex). Key for ad creative, social posts, product shots. Prompt engineering matters enormously here.
  • Video generation — Runway, Pika, Sora. Emerging but fast-moving. Best for short-form social content, product demos, motion graphics.
  • Voice & audio — ElevenLabs, OpenAI TTS, Google Cloud TTS, Play.ht. Voiceovers, podcast intros, ad narration. Cloning + multilingual are the unlock.
  • Music & sound — Suno, Udio, MusicGen. Background tracks, jingles, soundscapes. Still early but usable for non-licensed content needs.

What Matters in Practice

Brand Guardrails

Every generation step needs access to brand voice, colour palettes, dos and don'ts. RAG over brand docs is the standard approach — embed guidelines, retrieve per-prompt.

Human-in-the-Loop

Full automation isn't the goal — augmented speed is. Low-risk outputs (internal drafts) auto-approve; high-risk (client-facing, legal) require human sign-off.

Cost Control

Image and video models are 10–100x the cost of text. Route drafts through cheaper models, reserve premium for finals. Budget caps per-project, not per-model.

Evaluation

You can't improve what you can't measure. Track brand alignment scores, revision rates, time-to-publish, and client approval rates across the pipeline.

Example: Social Campaign

A concrete walkthrough of how this plays out for a 10-post social media campaign.

  • Brief intake: Brand uploads campaign brief (audience, goals, tone, platforms). System extracts structured parameters.
  • Context pull: RAG retrieves brand guidelines + top-performing past posts + competitor examples from knowledge base.
  • Concept generation: LLM produces 15 post concepts with hooks, CTAs, and visual direction notes. Creative director agent ranks top 10.
  • Asset production: Copy agent writes final captions. Image agent generates visuals (2–3 variants each). Runs in parallel.
  • Review: QA agent checks brand compliance, tone, and platform specs. Flags 2 posts for human review. Rest auto-approved.
  • Publish: Approved assets pushed to scheduling tool. Performance data feeds back for next campaign's RAG context.
