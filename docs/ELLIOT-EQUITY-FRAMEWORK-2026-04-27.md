# Elliot / Foundations — Equity Partnership Framework

**Context:** Elliot Cohen (Co-Founder, Foundations) and his partner have expressed interest in an equity-based model rather than straight commission/ARR percentage. Second call scheduled Monday April 28.

**Goal for Monday:** Understand what they're actually offering to do — don't negotiate numbers yet.

## Key Questions to Answer on the Call

1. **"What does the day-to-day look like for you on this — are you running sales full-time or making intros?"**
   - You need to understand if he's hands-on-keyboard or hands-off. An intro is worth a dinner, not equity.

2. **"What's the commitment level — is this one of many portfolio companies, or would Bonito be a primary focus?"**
   - Foundations has 11-50 employees and presumably multiple clients. If Bonito is 10% of his attention, he shouldn't get 10% of the company.

3. **"What does your partner bring to this?"**
   - Is the partner technical? Sales? Operational? Two people wanting equity is a bigger ask — need to understand the full picture.

4. **"What does success look like for you in 6 months? In 12?"**
   - Forces him to quantify. Vague answers ("build pipeline," "make introductions") = advisor. Specific answers ("close 5 enterprise deals," "$500K ARR") = GTM co-founder.

5. **"Have you done equity-based partnerships before? How were those structured?"**
   - Let him anchor first. His answer tells you what he thinks is normal and what he's gotten before.

## Three Tiers — Match the Equity to the Commitment

### Tier 1: Advisor / Intro Network
**What they do:**
- Open doors and make warm introductions to companies in their portfolio
- Join occasional calls to add credibility or make the intro warmer
- Provide GTM advice — positioning, pricing feedback, who to target
- Light touch — maybe 2-5 hours per month
- Not running any sales process, not closing deals, not accountable for revenue

**What this is worth:**
- 0.5% – 1.0% equity
- 2-year vesting, monthly or quarterly
- No cliff (they're not full-time, cliff is overkill)
- Consider a 12-month sunset clause: if no intros convert to pipeline in 12 months, vesting stops

**Why this range:** Industry standard for startup advisors is 0.25%–1%. An advisor with a warm network in your ICP (fintech, Series A/B) is on the higher end. But this is still just access to a rolodex — the heavy lifting is on you.

**Watch out for:** If they want Tier 1 effort with Tier 2 or 3 equity. "I'll make some intros" is not a $500K equity grant.

---

### Tier 2: Part-Time GTM Lead
**What they do:**
- Actively build and manage a sales pipeline for Bonito — not just intros, but qualifying leads, running discovery calls, sending proposals
- Spend 15-25 hours/week on Bonito (not exclusive, but significant)
- Own a target list of accounts and work them systematically
- May bring in junior reps from Foundations to help with outreach
- Run the sales cycle but you (Shabari) still do technical demos and close
- Report on pipeline weekly — deals in progress, stage, expected close dates

**What this is worth:**
- 2% – 3% equity
- 4-year vesting, 1-year cliff
- Tie vesting acceleration to revenue milestones:
  - Example: standard monthly vesting, but if they hit $250K ARR within 12 months, an extra 0.5% accelerates immediately
  - If they miss milestones for 2 consecutive quarters, vesting pauses (not cancels — resumes when they're back on track)
- Consider adding a small commission kicker: 5-10% on deals they personally close, on top of equity. This keeps them hungry deal-to-deal.

**Why this range:** They're taking on real work and real accountability, but they're not all-in. They still have Foundations and other clients. 2-3% reflects meaningful contribution without treating them as a co-founder.

**Watch out for:** Make sure "part-time" is actually part-time. If they say 15 hours/week but deliver 2, you've given up equity for nothing. The milestone-based vesting protects you here.

---

### Tier 3: Full-Time GTM Co-Founder
**What they do:**
- Own the entire revenue function — pipeline generation, sales cycle, closing, customer success handoff
- Full-time commitment to Bonito — they either leave Foundations or Bonito becomes Foundations' primary focus
- Hire and manage sales reps as you scale
- Build the sales playbook, collateral, pricing strategy, competitive positioning
- Accountable for revenue targets — $500K+ ARR in year one, path to $1M+
- Sit in on strategic decisions, investor calls, pricing discussions
- Represent Bonito at conferences, on calls, in the market

**What this is worth:**
- 5% – 10% equity
- 4-year vesting, 1-year cliff
- Revenue milestones baked into the agreement:
  - Cliff only clears if $100K ARR (or 3 closed deals) achieved by month 12
  - Full vesting schedule assumes continued revenue growth targets (set annually)
  - If they leave or underperform, unvested shares return
- May include a modest base salary or stipend ($3-5K/month) if they're truly full-time and giving up Foundations income
- Commission on top: 10-15% on deals they close, paid on cash received

**Why this range:** This is a co-founder-level commitment. 5-10% is standard for a GTM co-founder joining post-product at a pre-revenue or early-revenue startup. The range depends on how much risk they're taking (leaving their company vs. pivoting their company toward Bonito).

**Watch out for:**
- If they want 10% but won't commit full-time — that's Tier 2 dressed up as Tier 3
- If they want equity AND a large salary ($10K+/month) — they're de-risking themselves completely while you take all the risk. Equity is compensation for risk. If there's no risk, there should be less equity.
- If both Elliot AND his partner want co-founder equity — that's 10-20% total, which is significant. Push back: one of them should be the Bonito point person, the other is advisory at best.

## Negotiation Guardrails

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| **Don't name a number first** | Let him anchor. His ask tells you how he values his own contribution. |
| **Never give equity without vesting** | If he walks after 3 months, you want to claw it back. Standard 4-year vest, 1-year cliff. |
| **Tie equity to outcomes, not time** | Pure time-based vesting rewards showing up, not performing. Add revenue milestones. |
| **No exclusivity without proof** | If he wants to be your only GTM channel, he needs to prove he can deliver first. 6-month trial. |
| **Separate Elliot from his partner** | Don't negotiate one pool for "Foundations." Understand each person's role and equity individually. |
| **Keep commission in the mix** | Even with equity, a commission component (5-15% on closed deals) keeps incentives tight. Equity alone can make people lazy because the payout is years away. |
| **Get it in writing before work starts** | Handshake equity deals blow up. Simple agreement: role, equity amount, vesting schedule, milestones, termination terms. Doesn't need to be a 50-page contract — a 2-page term sheet works. |
| **90-day out clause** | Both sides can walk away in the first 90 days with no equity owed. Protects everyone. |
